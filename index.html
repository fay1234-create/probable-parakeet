We arrive loud.

Coats off, glitter dusting the air. Someone yells, “shots?” Someone else yells “obviously!” The floor thumps like a heartbeat we all share.

Drinks clatter, laughter spills.

“You look incredible.” “What?” “I said incredible!” The bass drowns everything. We shout over the sound, half words, half rhythm.

Someone’s taking a photo—

the flash splits the night open. A reach, a laugh, a stumble—

—a fall. Silence that isn’t silence.

“Hey—are you okay?” “You okay?” “Hey!” The lights still spin but slower now. The music seems to wait for an answer.

Hands on shoulders. Breath held. “You’re fine right?” “He’s fine.” “He’s—”
